
# 💰 Black Money Transaction Analysis Dashboard (Power BI)

This project presents an interactive **Black Money Transaction Analysis Dashboard** built in **Power BI**, designed to uncover hidden patterns, assess risk levels, and deliver actionable insights into financial transactions.

---

## 📊 Project Overview

The dashboard analyzes large-scale transaction data to identify trends, high-risk activities, and key contributors to black money flow. With dynamic filters and interactive visuals, it enables deeper exploration and faster decision-making.

---

## 🚀 Key Features

### 📌 KPI Highlights

* **Total Black Money:** $25.02B analyzed across transactions
* **Average Risk Score:** 5.53 (Moderate Risk)
* **Top Industry:** Finance ($3.73B)
* **Top Country:** China (Highest transaction volume)
* **Total Transactions:** 10,000+

---

### 📈 Year-wise Transaction Trend

* Tracks growth of transaction amounts over time
* Highlights a consistent rise in suspicious financial activity

---

### 🏭 Industry Distribution Analysis

* Key contributors: Finance, Construction, Arms Trade, Luxury Goods
* Helps identify high-risk industries

---

### 💳 Transaction Type Analysis

* Major transaction types include:

  * Property Purchase
  * Stocks Transfer
  * Offshore Transfer
  * Cash Withdrawal
  * Cryptocurrency

---

### 🌍 Country-wise Insights

* Top countries by transaction volume:

  * China
  * Brazil
  * UAE
  * UK
  * Singapore

---

### ⚠️ Risk Score Distribution

* Segmented into:

  * Low Risk (0–3)
  * Moderate Risk (3–7)
  * High Risk (7–10)
* Majority fall under **Moderate to High Risk**

---

### 🏢 Shell Companies & Tax Havens

* Identifies frequently used shell companies
* Highlights tax havens such as:

  * Singapore
  * Bahamas
  * Switzerland

---

### 🔍 High-Value Transactions

* Displays top transactions based on amount, country, industry, and risk score

---

### 💡 Smart Insights

* Finance industry contributes the highest black money flow
* Property purchase is the most frequent transaction type
* China leads in transaction volume
* Shell companies and offshore accounts are key laundering channels
